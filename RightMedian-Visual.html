CM = AM = BM = ½ AB

Median to the Hypotenuse

In a right triangle, the median drawn from the right angle to the hypotenuse is exactly half the hypotenuse:

CM = ½ AB  (so CM = AM = BM)

Its endpoint M — the midpoint of the hypotenuse — is therefore the same distance from all three vertices.

Why it's true

The hypotenuse AB is a diameter of the triangle's circumscribed circle (the right angle "sees" a diameter, by Thales' theorem). Its centre is the midpoint M, and CM, AM, BM are all radii — hence equal, each half of AB.

It runs both ways

Conversely, if a triangle's median to one side equals half that side, the angle opposite is a right angle — a quick right-angle test.

Handy uses

It locates a right triangle's circumcentre instantly (the hypotenuse's midpoint) and is a staple step in coordinate-geometry and olympiad proofs.
