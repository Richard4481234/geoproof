Complementary & Supplementary Angles

Two angles are named by what they add up to:

complementary = add to 90° · supplementary = add to 180°

Here two angles share a middle ray. Drag that ray to trade size between them — their total stays fixed by the mode.

Complementary (90°)

The outer arms make a right angle, so the two pieces always sum to 90°. Each angle is the other's complement — e.g. 30° and 60°.

Supplementary (180°)

The outer arms make a straight line, so the two pieces always sum to 180°. Each angle is the other's supplement — e.g. 110° and 70°. Two angles in a straight line like this are also called a linear pair.

Handy to remember

"C" comes before "S", and 90 before 180 — Complementary is the smaller (90°), Supplementary the larger (180°). These pairings are the backbone of angle-chasing: a right angle or a straight line instantly gives you the other angle.
