Area of a Regular Polygon

A regular polygon (all sides and angles equal) has an apothem a — the perpendicular distance from the centre to the middle of a side. Its area is:

Area = ½ · a · P  (P = perimeter)

Equivalently, with n sides of length s, Area = ½·a·(n·s).

Why it works

Join the centre to every corner. This splits the polygon into n identical triangles, each with base s (a side) and height a (the apothem). Each has area ½·a·s, and n of them give ½·a·(n·s) = ½·a·P.

It becomes a circle

As n grows, the apothem approaches the radius R and the perimeter approaches the circumference 2πR, so the area ½·a·P approaches ½·R·2πR = πR². Step n up and watch the polygon hug the circle.

Useful relations

With circumradius R: side s = 2R·sin(180°/n), apothem a = R·cos(180°/n). So everything follows from R and n.
