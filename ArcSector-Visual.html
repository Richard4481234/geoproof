Arc Length & Sector Area

A central angle θ cuts off a slice of a circle. The arc and the sector are each just a fraction — θ out of the full 360° — of the whole circle:

arc length = (θ/360) · 2πr
sector area = (θ/360) · πr²

So a 90° slice is one-quarter of the circumference and one-quarter of the area; a 180° slice is half of each, and so on.

In radians it's even simpler

If the angle is measured in radians (where a full turn is 2π), the formulas shrink to arc = rθ and sector area = ½r²θ. Radians are "built for" circle measure.

Arc vs. chord

The arc is the curved rim of the slice; don't confuse it with the straight chord joining the two endpoints, which is shorter.

Where it's used

Pie charts, gears and pulleys, the distance a wheel rolls, fields of view, and any "slice of a circle" calculation rely on these two formulas.
