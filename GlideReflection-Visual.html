Glide Reflection

A glide reflection is the composition of a reflection across a line m with a translation by a vector v that is parallel to m. The order doesn't matter — reflect-then-slide and slide-then-reflect give the same result.

glide = (reflect over m) + (slide by v ∥ m)

The fourth isometry

Reflection, rotation, and translation are the other three rigid motions of the plane. The glide reflection is the only one that reverses orientation (the image is "flipped") yet has no fixed point. The classic picture is a line of footprints: each step is the previous one reflected and slid forward.

The defining property

For every point P, the midpoint of P and its image P″ lies exactly on the mirror line m. Toggle "P→P″ midpoints" and watch the three midpoints sit on the line wherever you drag.

Apply it twice

Doing a glide reflection twice cancels the two reflections and leaves a pure translation by 2v — no flip, no rotation. Toggle "Apply twice" to see it.

Why it completes the set

Every rigid motion of the plane is one of exactly four kinds: translation, rotation, reflection, or glide reflection. This is the classification of plane isometries.
