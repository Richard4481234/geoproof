AA Similarity

If two angles of one triangle equal two angles of another, the triangles are similar — the same shape at (possibly) different sizes:

two equal angle pairs ⟹ triangles similar ⟹ sides in equal ratio

Two equal angles is enough, because the third angles must then match too (all three sum to 180°). That's why it's "AA," not "AAA."

What similar means

Similar triangles have all corresponding angles equal and all corresponding sides in one common ratio — the scale factor k. Here DEF is built with the same angles as ABC, so DE/AB = EF/BC = FD/CA, and the slider just changes that shared k.

Similar vs congruent

Congruent triangles are similar with k = 1 (same size). General similarity allows any k.

Why it's useful

AA is the quickest similarity test and the engine behind indirect measurement (heights from shadows), scale models, and a great deal of trigonometry — the trig ratios only make sense because all right triangles with a given acute angle are similar.
