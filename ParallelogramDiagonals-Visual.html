AM = MC  and  BM = MD

Diagonals Bisect Each Other

In every parallelogram, the two diagonals cross at their common midpoint:

AM = MC  and  BM = MD

Each diagonal cuts the other exactly in half, so their intersection M is the midpoint of both.

Why it's true

Opposite sides AB and DC are equal and parallel, which makes triangles ABM and CDM congruent (ASA). Matching sides then give AM = CM and BM = DM.

It works in reverse

The converse holds too: if a quadrilateral's diagonals bisect each other, it must be a parallelogram — a handy test, and the reason M is the figure's centre of symmetry.

Special cases

In a rectangle the diagonals are also equal; in a rhombus they're also perpendicular; in a square, both at once.
