Perpendicular from the Center Bisects a Chord

Drop a perpendicular from a circle's center O to any chord AB. It always strikes the chord at its midpoint:

OM ⊥ AB  ⟹  AM = MB

Equivalently, the line from the center to a chord's midpoint is perpendicular to that chord — the statement works in both directions.

Why it's true

OA and OB are both radii, so triangle OAB is isosceles. The perpendicular from the apex O of an isosceles triangle to its base lands on the base's midpoint — so AM = MB.

The right-triangle bonus

OM, AM and the radius OA form a right triangle, giving r² = OM² + (½AB)². So the farther a chord sits from the center, the shorter it is — and the longest chord (through the center) is the diameter.

Where it's used

This is the standard way to locate a circle's center (intersect two chords' perpendicular bisectors) and underlies many compass-and-straightedge constructions.
