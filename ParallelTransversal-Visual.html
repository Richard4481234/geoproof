Parallel Lines & a Transversal

When a line (the transversal) crosses two parallel lines, it makes the same angle with each one. That single fact produces the classic angle relationships:

corresponding = equal · alternate interior = equal · co-interior = 180°

Corresponding angles

Angles in the same position at each crossing (e.g. both top-right) are equal. Here every angle of one colour equals every other angle of that colour.

Alternate interior angles

The two angles between the parallel lines, on opposite sides of the transversal, are equal.

Co-interior (same-side interior) angles

The two angles between the parallel lines on the same side of the transversal add up to 180° — they are supplementary. (These are the differently-coloured interior pair.)

Why it all works

Because the lines are parallel, the transversal cuts both at the identical angle, so each crossing shows the very same four angles — one value and its 180° supplement. Drag a handle to change the slant and watch both crossings stay in lockstep.
