Equal Chords, Equal Distances

In a circle, two chords have the same length if and only if they are the same distance from the centre:

AB = CD  ⟺  d₁ = d₂

The distance is measured along the perpendicular from the centre to the chord.

Why it works

The perpendicular from the centre bisects a chord, so a chord of length L, its half L/2, the distance d, and the radius R form a right triangle: (L/2)² + d² = R². Hence L = 2√(R² − d²). Equal L forces equal d, and vice versa.

Longer means closer

Because of that formula, a smaller distance d gives a longer chord. The longest chord of all — at distance 0 — is a diameter, passing right through the centre.

Where it's used

It's the reason equal arcs subtend equal chords, and it underlies constructions that compare or reproduce chord lengths with a compass.
